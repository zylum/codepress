# FAQ

## What is CodePress?

CodePress is a product development operating system for the AI era. It provides structure for turning ideas into shippable software while ensuring every delivery leaves behind reusable knowledge. Think of it as a delivery factory, knowledge system, and product framework rolled into one.

## How is it different from Jira?

Jira tracks work. CodePress shapes work before it is tracked, executes it in parallel atomic units, and captures learning afterwards. The difference is the compound loop — every cycle improves the next.

## How is it different from Shape Up?

Shape Up is about product shaping — deciding what to build and giving teams room to figure out how. CodePress includes shaping (the Galley stage) but adds structured delivery (Slugs), parallel build, and a knowledge feedback loop that Shape Up does not have.

## How is it different from Compound Engineering?

Compound Engineering is an AI delivery methodology — how to build software with AI efficiently. CodePress complements it with a product system (what to build) and a knowledge system (what was learned). They work together: Compound Engineering powers delivery, CodePress powers shaping and learning.

## How is it different from BMAD?

BMAD (Build, Measure, Learn) is a lean startup loop. CodePress is a development operating system. BMAD asks "are we building the right thing?" CodePress answers "how do we build it, ship it, and get smarter from it?"

## What's a Galley?

A Galley is a shaped solution — the thinking artefact. It captures the problem, users, success criteria, constraints, approach, and acceptance criteria. It is written before any code. The name comes from linotype printing, where a galley was the metal tray that held assembled type before it went to press.

## What's a Slug?

A Slug is an atomic unit of delivery. Each Slug has a clear purpose, an acceptance checklist, a defined autonomy level, and produces a single commit. Slugs are designed to be built independently and in parallel.

## What's the compound loop?

The loop that improves the entire system over time:

```text
Signal → Pattern → Knowledge
```

Raw observations from delivery (Signals) are promoted to validated reusable lessons (Patterns) that update shared understanding (Knowledge). Every cycle leaves the organisation smarter.

## Do I need to use the CLI?

No. The CLI is a helper, not a requirement. All skills are plain markdown files. You can follow them manually in any AI agent.

## Can I customise the structure?

Yes. The three pillars (System, Product, Knowledge) are the foundation, but you can add or reorganise within them. The system is designed to adapt to your project, not the other way around.
